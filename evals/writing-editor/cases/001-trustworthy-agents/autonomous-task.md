# Autonomous Editorial Pilot

Use `$writing-editor` to develop a serious, source-grounded article from the supplied source.

## Assignment

The broad subject has already been selected: production reliability for AI agents.

The source is `input/source.pdf`; an identical deterministic extraction is available at `input/source.txt`. Treat the source as the foundation, not as the article outline and not as something to summarize.

The article should help readers who already have an agent, demo, pilot, or early production workflow understand what must exist around that agent before it can be trusted with customers, data, and operations as usage grows. Translate technical mechanisms into reader-facing value. Remain vendor-neutral and do not market the source company or claim first-hand experience.

The reader is problem-locked rather than persona-locked. Begin simply, become technically substantive, and make the application useful. Combine avoidable downside with a credible positive future state. Do not widen the piece merely to cover every source topic.

## Scope

- Produce the article only. Do not create an image, social post, or distribution package.
- Title work may remain provisional; prioritize the article's argument and body.
- Use `output/` as the only article workspace. Do not access or modify files outside this trial directory.
- No live web research is needed; the source packet is frozen for this comparison.
- The author is unavailable during this non-interactive pilot. You are authorized to make reasonable, reversible editorial assumptions where the supplied direction is sufficient. Record material assumptions instead of stopping for confirmation.
- Do not call the article approved, published, or final on the user's behalf. Leave the strongest result ready for review.

Create the workspace and complete the editorial work through a best ready-for-review article. Preserve enough observable state to inspect the direction, source use, critique, and revision decisions.
